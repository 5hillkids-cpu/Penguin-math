# Detailed Lesson Plan

## Lesson Information
- Title: Industrial Chiller Control and Diagnostics
- Duration: 5.5 hours
- Delivery mode: Instructor-led with digital reinforcement

## Competencies Addressed
- Interpret chiller sequence logic and protection states.
- Use sensor, alarm, and controller data to isolate lockout causes.
- Distinguish actual process faults from false instrumentation signals.

## Learning Objectives
1. Describe the compressor enable sequence.
2. Explain how key chiller safeties prevent equipment damage.
3. Diagnose a low-temperature lockout using available evidence.
4. Document the corrective path and restart verification.

## Lesson Opener
Prompt: “The building needs chilled water, pumps are running, and the chiller still refuses to start because of a low-temperature lockout. Do you reset it, replace a part, or prove what the controller believes first?”

## Instructional Sequence

### Phase 1: Chiller Orientation, 35 minutes
- Review major components and controller responsibilities.
- Check for understanding: Identify the minimum permissives required before compressor enable.

### Phase 2: Safeties and Sensors, 45 minutes
- Discuss leaving-water temperature, flow proof, pressure safeties, and lockout logic.
- Check for understanding: Match each safety to the equipment risk it protects against.

### Phase 3: Guided Diagnostics, 45 minutes
- Instructor compares real process conditions with controller points.
- Check for understanding: Learners explain why an alarm may be valid logic acting on bad data.

### Phase 4: Workbook Practice, 30 minutes
- Learners fill in evidence tables and justify their next test step.

### Phase 5: Virtual Lab, 55 minutes
- Learners diagnose the failed sensor scenario and propose verification steps.

### Phase 6: Debrief and Assessment, 20 minutes
- Review common misdiagnoses, then complete exit assessment.
