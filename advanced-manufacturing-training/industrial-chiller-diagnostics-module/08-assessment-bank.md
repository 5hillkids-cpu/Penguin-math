# Assessment Bank

## Module Title
Industrial Chiller Control and Diagnostics

## Knowledge Check Questions

### Multiple Choice
1. Which evidence most strongly suggests the chiller lockout is caused by bad sensor data rather than real freeze risk?
   - A. Cooling demand is true
   - B. Handheld water temperature is much warmer than the controller reading
   - C. Compressor enable output is false
   - D. Pump proof is true
   - Correct answer: B

2. In the scenario, why is the controller preventing compressor enable?
   - A. It ignores BAS demand
   - B. It is incorrectly programmed at random
   - C. It sees a low leaving-water temperature condition and applies protection logic
   - D. It always requires manual override first
   - Correct answer: C

3. What is the best next step before resetting the lockout?
   - A. Replace the compressor
   - B. Verify sensor plausibility and signal integrity
   - C. Disable the temperature safety
   - D. Restart repeatedly until it clears
   - Correct answer: B

## Short Answer
1. Why can a controller make the correct decision based on incorrect input data?
2. Explain the difference between a real low-temperature event and a false low-temperature signal.
3. What evidence supports replacing or recalibrating the leaving-water sensor?

## Performance Task
Given a chiller lockout scenario, the learner must:
1. State the symptom.
2. Identify the key conflicting data points.
3. Explain why the logic response is still reasonable.
4. Recommend a safe corrective and verification process.
