# Industrial Chiller Control and Diagnostics

## Module Overview
- Topic area: Chiller electrical systems, safeties, instrumentation, PLC or controller logic, and sequence-based fault diagnosis
- Target audience: Industrial maintenance technicians, HVAC controls technicians, utilities operators, and mechatronics learners
- Recommended duration: 5.5 hours instructor-led plus 60 minutes digital prework
- Delivery format: Blended technical instruction with digital module, guided diagnostics, and scenario lab
- Prerequisites: Electrical safety, meter use, basic refrigeration-cycle awareness, and control wiring familiarity
- Required tools and materials: Chiller sequence-of-operation document, control schematic, alarm list, sensor point table, BAS screenshots, and sample pressure-temperature data

## Safety Requirements
- Apply lockout-tagout before opening control cabinets.
- Use site-qualified personnel and procedures for refrigerant systems.
- Treat pumps, compressors, and rotating equipment as stored-energy hazards.
- Do not bypass safeties during instruction except in documented simulation only.

## Learning Objectives
1. Explain the normal start and protection sequence of an industrial chiller.
2. Interpret safety, sensor, and alarm inputs that prevent compressor operation.
3. Differentiate between electrical, instrumentation, and process-condition causes of chiller lockout.
4. Diagnose a no-start or trip event using alarm history, controller state, and measured values.
5. Verify corrective action without compromising equipment protection.

## Module Scenario Theme
The chilled-water system has demand, but the chiller will not enable the compressor. The controller reports a lockout after a low-evaporator-temperature event. Learners must determine whether the issue is a failed sensor, process condition, safety circuit, or controller logic response.
