# Georgia Mechanical Master Implementation Guide

## Purpose
This guide explains how Georgia Mechanical can deploy the Advanced Manufacturing Troubleshooting Series across instructors, LMS administrators, supervisors, and technical leads.

## Program Components
- Branded course flyers for each module
- Instructor manuals and lesson plans
- Learner workbooks
- Virtual labs and assessment banks
- Rise production scripts and Storyline scene scripts
- Facilitator slide scripts and delivery guides
- Final exam and supervisor signoff tools

## Recommended Rollout Model

### Phase 1: Preparation
1. Select the target learner group.
2. Match each module to technician roles and skill levels.
3. Replace generic screenshots, tag names, and diagrams with Georgia Mechanical or client-specific examples.
4. Load LMS shell content and enrollment copy.

### Phase 2: Facilitator Readiness
1. Review instructor manuals and Georgia Mechanical facilitator guides.
2. Build presentation materials from the branded slide scripts.
3. Confirm virtual lab format and delivery logistics.
4. Align supervisors on post-training observation expectations.

### Phase 3: Learner Delivery
1. Assign digital prework through Rise or Storyline.
2. Deliver instructor-led classroom or lab session.
3. Complete workbook and scenario lab activities.
4. Administer module assessments and capstone evaluation.

### Phase 4: Post-Training Verification
1. Use supervisor observation checklists during field follow-up.
2. Complete final exam and signoff process.
3. Identify technicians needing coaching, retraining, or advanced assignments.

## Role Responsibilities

### Training Manager
- Schedule sessions
- Track completions
- Approve facilitator assignments
- Maintain branded program materials

### Facilitator
- Deliver module content
- Run discussion and lab activities
- Evaluate learner reasoning and participation
- Report gaps for follow-up coaching

### LMS Administrator
- Publish course shells
- Track enrollment and completion
- Maintain certificates and digital assets

### Supervisor
- Observe on-the-job application
- Use checklists and signoff forms
- Reinforce Georgia Mechanical troubleshooting standards in the field

## Recommended Delivery Sequence
1. Conveyor System Electrical and PLC Troubleshooting
2. Industrial HVAC Electrical and Control Troubleshooting
3. Industrial Chiller Control and Diagnostics

## Suggested Timeline
- Week 1: Conveyor module
- Week 2: HVAC module
- Week 3: Chiller module
- Week 4: Capstone assessment and supervisor observation

## Success Measures
- Completion rate
- Assessment pass rate
- Supervisor signoff rate
- Reduced repeat faults or unnecessary part replacements
- Improved technician reasoning during troubleshooting discussions

## Deployment Checklist
- Course flyers distributed
- LMS shells published
- Facilitator guides reviewed
- Slide materials built
- Workbooks printed or distributed digitally
- Labs scheduled
- Final exam assigned
- Supervisor observations scheduled

The strongest rollout will keep one message consistent across every role: Georgia Mechanical expects technicians to troubleshoot with evidence, sequence logic, safe correction, and verified restart, not guesswork.
