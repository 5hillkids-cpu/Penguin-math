# Master Instructor Guide

## Course Title
Advanced Manufacturing Troubleshooting Series

## Course Purpose
This guide combines three standalone technical modules into a multi-day troubleshooting series focused on electrical systems, instrumentation, PLC logic, and industrial mechanical reasoning. The course is designed for maintenance technicians, mechatronics apprentices, and facilities support staff who must diagnose industrial equipment faults safely and systematically.

## Included Modules
1. Conveyor System Electrical and PLC Troubleshooting
2. Industrial HVAC Electrical and Control Troubleshooting
3. Industrial Chiller Control and Diagnostics

## Recommended Delivery Model
- Format: Three-day instructor-led course with digital prework and scenario labs
- Audience size: 8 to 16 learners
- Training environment: Classroom plus equipment trainer, simulation station, or digital lab environment

## Program-Level Outcomes
By the end of the series, learners will be able to:

1. Interpret control sequences across multiple industrial systems.
2. Use field evidence, controller status, and measurements to isolate faults.
3. Distinguish input-side, logic-side, output-side, and process-condition failures.
4. Document root cause, corrective action, and restart verification clearly.
5. Apply safe troubleshooting habits under production pressure.

## Suggested Agenda

### Day 1: Conveyor Controls and PLC Troubleshooting
- Morning: Sequence of operation, PLC inputs and outputs, permissive logic
- Midday: Guided conveyor troubleshooting demonstration
- Afternoon: Workbook activity and virtual lab

### Day 2: Industrial HVAC Control Troubleshooting
- Morning: BAS demand, safety strings, control voltage path
- Midday: Guided fan-start fault isolation
- Afternoon: Workbook activity and virtual lab

### Day 3: Chiller Diagnostics and Protected Equipment Logic
- Morning: Enable logic, alarms, sensor plausibility, lockouts
- Midday: Guided chiller lockout diagnosis
- Afternoon: Workbook activity, virtual lab, and final capstone discussion

## Instructor Preparation
- Review site-specific equipment examples before delivery.
- Substitute generic tags, point names, and alarms with local plant references where possible.
- Confirm training aids, schematics, and screenshots match the lessons.
- Prepare one normal-state and one fault-state example for each module.

## Materials Checklist
- Printed learner workbooks for all modules
- Instructor manuals and lesson plans
- Schematics, I/O lists, and controller screenshots
- Multimeters and diagnostic reference tools
- Whiteboard or digital markup tool
- Access to Rise modules or projector-based storyboard walkthroughs

## Facilitation Strategy
- Push learners to justify every conclusion with evidence.
- Require them to state what the system should be doing before naming what is wrong.
- Use the same common troubleshooting framework in every module:
  1. Confirm the symptom
  2. Review normal sequence of operation
  3. Identify missing permissive, safety, or process condition
  4. Verify with measurements or controller data
  5. Correct the root cause
  6. Verify stable restart

## Assessment Plan
- Module-level knowledge checks inside each package
- Performance-based virtual lab assessment in each module
- End-of-series capstone discussion or practical demonstration

## Capstone Prompt
Present learners with three short failure cases, one conveyor, one HVAC unit, and one chiller. Ask them to identify:
1. The symptom
2. The likely blocking condition
3. The next best diagnostic step
4. The safest corrective path

## Adaptation Notes
- For apprentices: Slow down the sequence-of-operation analysis and add more diagram-labeling practice.
- For experienced technicians: Increase ambiguity in the scenarios and reduce scaffolding.
- For blended delivery: Assign Rise modules as prework and reserve classroom time for discussion and labs.
