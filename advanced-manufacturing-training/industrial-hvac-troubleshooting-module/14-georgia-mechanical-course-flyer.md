# Georgia Mechanical Course Flyer

## Course Title
Georgia Mechanical: Industrial HVAC Electrical and Control Troubleshooting

## Course Snapshot
Trace HVAC control voltage, safety strings, and relay logic to diagnose no-fan-start and control-path faults without unnecessary part replacement.

## Who Should Attend
- HVAC technicians
- Maintenance technicians
- Facilities support personnel
- Technical apprentices

## What Learners Will Be Able To Do
- Explain the HVAC fan-start sequence
- Distinguish demand, permission, and command
- Trace low-voltage control paths
- Identify blocking safety devices
- Verify safe restart after correction

## Training Format
- Instructor-led technical session
- Guided troubleshooting workbook
- Virtual lab scenario
- Rise or Storyline digital reinforcement

## Estimated Duration
5 hours instructor-led plus digital reinforcement

## Why Georgia Mechanical Uses This Course
This course helps technicians isolate HVAC faults faster, reduce repeat service calls, and strengthen evidence-based troubleshooting across BAS-controlled equipment.

## Key Topic Areas
- BAS demand versus equipment permission
- Safety strings and interlocks
- Control transformer and relay logic
- No-start fault isolation
- Restart verification

## Takeaway
Technicians leave with a Georgia Mechanical troubleshooting standard that prevents guessing and improves first-time fix quality.
