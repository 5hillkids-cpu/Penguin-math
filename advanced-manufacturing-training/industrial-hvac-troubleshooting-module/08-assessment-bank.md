# Assessment Bank

## Module Title
Industrial HVAC Electrical and Control Troubleshooting

## Knowledge Check Questions

### Multiple Choice
1. If the BAS command is true and the control transformer output is healthy, but the relay coil sees 0 VAC, the fault is most likely:
   - A. In the safety or control path upstream of the relay
   - B. In the supply fan bearings only
   - C. In the BAS graphics page only
   - D. In the thermostat cover
   - Correct answer: A

2. Which device in the scenario most directly interrupted the start sequence?
   - A. Motor overload relay
   - B. Freezestat
   - C. Fan blade
   - D. Disconnect handle
   - Correct answer: B

3. What is the best reason not to replace the fan contactor first?
   - A. The relay coil never received control voltage
   - B. Contactors are never used in HVAC systems
   - C. BAS commands always bypass contactors
   - D. Freezestats cannot fail
   - Correct answer: A

## Short Answer
1. Explain why a BAS command is not enough by itself to prove the unit should start.
2. Describe how a safety string works in a control circuit.
3. Give two reasons a freezestat might be open.

## Performance Task
Given an HVAC no-fan-start scenario, the learner must:
1. Trace the control path.
2. Identify the blocking device or condition.
3. Explain why the relay or contactor was not the first proven failure.
4. Recommend a safe corrective action and restart check.
