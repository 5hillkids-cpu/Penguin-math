# Detailed Lesson Plan

## Lesson Information
- Title: Industrial HVAC Electrical and Control Troubleshooting
- Duration: 5 hours
- Delivery mode: Instructor-led with digital reinforcement

## Competencies Addressed
- Read HVAC control schematics and sequences of operation.
- Verify safeties, relays, sensors, and outputs in a running or faulted system.
- Diagnose no-start and airflow-control problems using measured evidence.

## Learning Objectives
1. Describe the control path for a fan-start call.
2. Explain the purpose of common HVAC safeties and interlocks.
3. Isolate whether a fault is in the power path, control path, or mechanical equipment.
4. Document the diagnostic path and corrective action.

## Lesson Opener
Prompt: “The BAS says the unit should be running, the space is warming up, and the fan is still off. Which checks tell you most quickly whether the unit is blocked by safety logic or failed hardware?”

## Instructional Sequence

### Phase 1: Unit Orientation, 35 minutes
- Review main components and control path.
- Check for understanding: Identify the order of control devices before the fan contactor coil.

### Phase 2: Safeties and Sensors, 40 minutes
- Discuss freezestat, airflow proof, overloads, and limit devices.
- Check for understanding: Classify each device as permissive, protection, or feedback.

### Phase 3: Guided Diagnostic Model, 45 minutes
- Instructor traces control voltage through the faulted circuit.
- Check for understanding: Learners explain where the voltage path stops and why.

### Phase 4: Workbook Practice, 30 minutes
- Learners complete guided notes and fault tables.
- Check for understanding: Learners justify their next test step.

### Phase 5: Virtual Lab, 50 minutes
- Learners work a no-fan-start scenario with BAS and meter data.
- Check for understanding: Learners identify root cause and verification method.

### Phase 6: Debrief and Assessment, 20 minutes
- Review common wrong turns and administer exit questions.

## Assessment Method
- Formative questioning, workbook completion, and live reasoning checks
- Summative knowledge check and virtual lab rubric
