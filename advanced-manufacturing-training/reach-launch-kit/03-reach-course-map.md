# Georgia Mechanical Reach Course Map

## Purpose
This document shows how to structure the Georgia Mechanical training content as online courses and learning paths inside Reach 360.

## Recommended Reach Structure

### Learning Path
Georgia Mechanical Technical Troubleshooting Academy

### Courses Inside The Learning Path
1. Georgia Mechanical: Conveyor System Electrical and PLC Troubleshooting
2. Georgia Mechanical: Industrial HVAC Electrical and Control Troubleshooting
3. Georgia Mechanical: Industrial Chiller Control and Diagnostics
4. Georgia Mechanical Technical Troubleshooting Academy Final Assessment

## Suggested Course Order
1. Conveyor
2. HVAC
3. Chiller
4. Final assessment and supervisor signoff

## Suggested Reach Metadata
- Audience: Georgia Mechanical technicians and apprentices
- Topic tags: troubleshooting, controls, PLC, HVAC, chiller, maintenance
- Difficulty: Intermediate
- Delivery type: blended or self-paced depending on deployment model

## Enrollment Recommendation
- Enroll new technicians automatically into the full learning path
- Assign experienced technicians into individual modules as refreshers
- Assign supervisors to the final assessment and signoff workflow only if they do not need the learner modules

## Reporting Recommendation
Track these milestones:
1. Course enrollment
2. Course completion
3. Final assessment completion
4. Supervisor signoff complete

## Reach Launch Sequence
1. Upload the conveyor course first as a pilot.
2. Validate learner launch, mobile behavior, and completion tracking.
3. Upload HVAC and chiller after the pilot passes review.
4. Add the final assessment and supervisor signoff path.
5. Group all items into the full Georgia Mechanical academy path.

## Practical Recommendation
If you want a clean first deployment, launch only the conveyor course first, collect feedback, then clone that pattern for HVAC and chiller instead of building all three in Reach at the same time.
