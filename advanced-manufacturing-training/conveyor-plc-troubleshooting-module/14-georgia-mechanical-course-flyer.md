# Georgia Mechanical Course Flyer

## Course Title
Georgia Mechanical: Conveyor System Electrical and PLC Troubleshooting

## Course Snapshot
Troubleshoot conveyor no-start and no-restart faults using PLC logic, permissive analysis, field-device verification, and safe restart practices.

## Who Should Attend
- Maintenance technicians
- Mechatronics apprentices
- Line support technicians
- Operations support staff

## What Learners Will Be Able To Do
- Explain the conveyor start sequence
- Identify missing permissives and interlocks
- Interpret PLC input and output evidence
- Diagnose jam-clear and no-restart faults
- Verify corrective action before returning equipment to service

## Training Format
- Instructor-led technical session
- Workbook-based guided practice
- Virtual troubleshooting lab
- Rise or Storyline digital reinforcement

## Estimated Duration
4.5 hours instructor-led plus digital reinforcement

## Why Georgia Mechanical Uses This Course
This course helps technicians reduce downtime, avoid unnecessary part replacement, and apply a repeatable troubleshooting method under production pressure.

## Key Topic Areas
- Sequence of operation
- PLC inputs and outputs
- Permissives and interlocks
- Fault-zone isolation
- Sensor verification and restart confirmation

## Takeaway
Technicians leave with a practical Georgia Mechanical troubleshooting method: confirm the symptom, review the sequence, identify the missing condition, verify with data, correct the cause, and verify the restart.
