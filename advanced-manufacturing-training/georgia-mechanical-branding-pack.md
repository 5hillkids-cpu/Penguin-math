## Program Description
Georgia Mechanical Technical Troubleshooting Academy is a branded training series designed to strengthen troubleshooting performance across industrial electrical, controls, instrumentation, and mechanical systems. The series uses realistic fault scenarios, instructor-led facilitation, digital learning modules, and virtual labs to help technicians diagnose problems with less guesswork and greater technical discipline.

## Audience Description
This series is intended for maintenance technicians, HVAC technicians, mechatronics apprentices, operations support personnel, and technical leads who must diagnose and restore industrial equipment safely and efficiently.

## Course Catalog Blurbs

### Conveyor System Electrical and PLC Troubleshooting
Georgia Mechanical technicians learn how to diagnose conveyor no-start and no-restart faults using PLC evidence, permissive logic, and field-device verification. The module emphasizes sequence-of-operation thinking, safe correction, and restart verification.

### Industrial HVAC Electrical and Control Troubleshooting
This Georgia Mechanical course teaches technicians how to trace control voltage, interpret safety strings, and isolate HVAC no-start conditions without unnecessary part replacement. Learners build a repeatable method for diagnosing the difference between demand, permission, and final command.

### Industrial Chiller Control and Diagnostics
This Georgia Mechanical module focuses on protected-equipment troubleshooting. Learners interpret alarms, compare controller inputs to field measurements, and diagnose whether a chiller lockout reflects a true process condition or a false sensor signal.

## Program Marketing Copy
Georgia Mechanical Technical Troubleshooting Academy gives industrial technicians a practical standard for diagnosing controlled equipment under real production pressure. Through blended lessons, scenario labs, and digital modules, learners build the habits needed to verify symptoms, trace signal paths, identify missing conditions, and restore equipment safely.
