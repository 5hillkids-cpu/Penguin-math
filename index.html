Hook Story

The trouble on the ice path

Penny the Penguin is delivering frozen fish pops, but she keeps stopping at random spots and shouting, "This feels like one-third!" The penguin team needs your help to split the ice path into equal parts and rescue snack time.

Learning Objective

I can place fractions on a number line by splitting the line into equal parts and counting from 0.

Slide 1

Fractions can live on a number line

Fractions are numbers. We can show them on a number line just like whole numbers. The space from 0 to 1 is one whole.

Slide 2

Equal parts matter

To show a fraction correctly, the space from 0 to 1 must be split into equal parts. Giant-medium-tiny parts do not work in fraction land.

Slide 5

First split 0 to 1 into 3 equal parts. Then count 2 parts from 0. That point is 2/3.

Place 2/3 on the number line

Tap the icy number line to place Penny. She will snap to the nearest third.

Earn 2 fish points for the correct stop.

Slide 6

Fractions can go past 1

A number line can keep going after 1. If the line is split into halves, then 2/2 is 1 whole and 3/2 is 1 whole and 1/2 more.

2/2 = 1 whole
+ 1/2 more
0 1/2 1 = 2/2 3/2 2

The line keeps going after 1, so 3/2 lands one half-step past the whole.

See it: 0 to 1 is two half-steps. One more half-step lands at 3/2.

Peaches says, “More fish pops after 1? This is my kind of math.”

Wrap-Up

You rescued snack time

Fractions are numbers. Split the whole into equal parts, use the denominator to know how many parts to make, and use the numerator to count how far to go from 0.
